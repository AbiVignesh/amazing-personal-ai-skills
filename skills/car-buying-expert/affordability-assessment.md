# Framework: Affordability Assessment Model

## Part 1: Financial Health Assessment

### Step 1: Income Analysis

**Question**: What's your take-home monthly salary?
- Include: Fixed salary, predictable bonuses
- Exclude: Variable income (less than 80% guaranteed)
- Calculate: After-tax, after-PF, after-deductions

**Rule**: Never use gross salary. Always use actual take-home.

```
Example:
Gross salary: ₹60k
PF contribution: ₹6k
Taxes: ₹8k
Other deductions: ₹2k
TAKE-HOME: ₹44k
```

---

### Step 2: Existing Obligations

**List All**:
- Home EMI (if any)
- Personal loan EMI
- Credit card minimum payment
- Rent (if paying)
- Insurance premiums
- Any other fixed obligations

**Calculate**: Total monthly obligations

```
Example:
Home loan EMI: ₹15k
Personal loan: ₹3k
Rent: ₹8k
Insurance: ₹1.5k
TOTAL: ₹27.5k
```

---

### Step 3: Essential Expenses

**Fixed Monthly Expenses**:
- Groceries: ₹8-12k
- Utilities (water, electricity, internet): ₹2-3k
- Phone/services: ₹1-2k
- Insurance (health, life): ₹1-2k
- Others: ₹2-3k

**Total Essential**: ₹14-22k (varies by city, family size)

```
Example (Family of 4):
Groceries: ₹10k
Utilities: ₹2.5k
Phone/Internet: ₹1.5k
Insurance: ₹1.5k
Miscellaneous: ₹2.5k
TOTAL: ₹18k
```

---

### Step 4: Available Margin

**Calculation**:
```
Take-home salary: ₹44k
- Existing EMI: ₹18.5k
- Essential expenses: ₹18k
= AVAILABLE: ₹7.5k
```

**This is your safe car EMI limit** (if only 1 obligation; less if multiple).

## Part 2: EMI Calculation & Safety Rules

### EMI Formula

```
EMI = [P × r × (1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]

Where:
P = Loan amount
r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12)
n = Number of months

Example:
Loan: ₹25L (₹25,00,000)
Rate: 8% per annum = 0.667% per month
Tenure: 60 months

EMI = [2500000 × 0.00667 × (1.00667)^60] / [(1.00667)^60 - 1]
    = ₹61,143 per month

Total interest paid: ₹(61,143 × 60) - 25,00,000 = ₹1,16,580
Total repayment: ₹41,16,580
```

**Online Calculator**: Use https://emicalculator.in or dealer's EMI calculator.

---

### Safety Ratios (India-Specific)

**Ratio 1: EMI-to-Take-Home**
```
SAFE: EMI ≤ 15% of take-home
STRETCHING: EMI 15-20% of take-home
DANGEROUS: EMI > 20% of take-home

Example (Take-home ₹50k):
Safe EMI: ₹7,500
Stretching EMI: ₹7,500-10,000
Dangerous EMI: >₹10,000
```

**Ratio 2: Total Debt-to-Take-Home**
```
SAFE: All EMIs ≤ 30% of take-home
STRETCHING: All EMIs 30-40% of take-home
DANGEROUS: All EMIs > 40% of take-home

Example:
Take-home: ₹50k
Existing EMI: ₹8k
Car EMI safe max: ₹7.5k (so total = ₹15.5k = 31% total)
Car EMI dangerous threshold: >₹12k (so total > 40%)
```

**Ratio 3: Car Cost-to-Annual-Salary**
```
SAFE: Car price ≤ 1-1.5x annual take-home
STRETCHING: Car price 1.5-2x annual take-home
DANGEROUS: Car price > 2x annual take-home

Example (Take-home ₹50k = ₹6L/year):
Safe car price: ₹6-9L
Stretching: ₹9-12L
Dangerous: >₹12L
```

## Part 3: Car Affordability Decision Tree

```
START: What's your take-home monthly salary?

STEP 1: Calculate safe EMI limit
├─ Take-home × 15% = Safe EMI
├─ Take-home × 20% = Stretching EMI
└─ Take-home × 25% = Dangerous EMI

STEP 2: Determine max loan amount
├─ Safe EMI × 60 months (assuming ₹0 down payment, 8% rate)
├─ Adjust down payment if available
└─ Calculate max car price = Loan + Down payment

STEP 3: Check against ratios
├─ Is car price ≤ 1.5x annual salary?
├─ Is total debt ≤ 30% of take-home?
└─ Is emergency fund ≥ 3 months?

STEP 4: Make verdict
├─ All checks pass → PROCEED (car is affordable)
├─ Ratio stretches but >15% margin → PROCEED WITH CAUTION (monitor closely)
├─ Multiple ratios fail → CAUTION (consider smaller car)
└─ Multiple ratios in danger zone → STOP (don't buy)
```

## Part 4: Loan Comparison Framework

| Factor | Bank Loan | NBFC | Dealer Finance |
|--------|-----------|------|-----------------|
| Interest Rate | 7.5-9.5% | 9-12% | 10-14% |
| Processing Time | 7-10 days | 2-3 days | Instant |
| Down Payment | 10-20% | 20-25% | 25%+ |
| Tenure | 36-84 months | 36-84 months | 36-84 months |
| Flexibility | High (can change lender) | Locked to NBFC | Locked to dealer |
| Risk | Low (regulated) | Medium | Medium (tied to dealer) |
| Best For | Good credit score, time available | Quick approval needed | Convenience, poor credit |

**Recommendation**: Bank loans are usually best (lowest rate, most flexibility). NBFC if fast approval needed. Avoid dealer financing (highest cost).

## Part 5: Down Payment Strategy

### How Much to Pay Down?

**Formula**:
```
Minimize down payment if:
- Interest rate is ≤ 8% (invest surplus at 10% returns, save interest)
- You have good emergency fund (₹10L+)
- You have high savings rate (>20%)

Example:
Car price: ₹20L
Option 1: ₹5L down + ₹15L loan at 8% for 60 months
  EMI: ₹30.5k/month
  Interest cost: ₹8.3L
  Keep ₹5L as emergency fund + investments

Option 2: ₹10L down + ₹10L loan at 8% for 60 months
  EMI: ₹20.3k/month (more affordable)
  Interest cost: ₹5.2L
  But: Emergency fund reduced to ₹5L (risky if salary drops)

Choose Option 1 if salary stable & good emergency fund.
Choose Option 2 if monthly budget tight.
```

**Rule of Thumb**:
- If comfortable: Keep 6 months emergency fund + 1 year savings + then pay down
- If tight: Pay minimum down payment, preserve cash reserve

## Part 6: Tenure Decision (How Many Months?)

| Tenure | EMI (for ₹20L @ 8%) | Total Interest | Monthly Impact | Recommendation |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 36 months | ₹62.1k | ₹6.3L | High, stressful | Only if very comfortable |
| 48 months | ₹48.9k | ₹8.4L | Moderate | Good balance |
| 60 months | ₹40.8k | ₹10.5L | Comfortable | Default choice |
| 72 months | ₹35.3k | ₹12.6L | Easier now, hard later | Avoid |
| 84 months | ₹31.3k | ₹15.0L | Too low to question | Avoid at all costs |

**Rule**: Choose 60 months as default. Only go shorter if very comfortable, never longer.

**Why**: 84-month loans tie you down for 7 years. Car depreciates 70%+. You're paying full price for a car worth 30% by year 7.

## Part 7: Affordability Red Flags

| Red Flag | What It Means | Action |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| EMI takes >20% of salary | Stretching financially | Reduce car price or tenure |
| Down payment <10% | High loan burden | Save more before buying |
| No emergency fund | One repair = debt crisis | Build 3-month fund first |
| Tenure >60 months | Paying for depreciating asset | Reduce car price instead |
| Monthly car cost > 25% of salary | Car owns you | Downgrade significantly |
| Can't explain the budget | Emotional buying | Take a week, recalculate |
| Using bonus/raises for EMI | Risky assumption | Use only take-home salary |
| Borrowing for down payment | Debt spiral | Save more first |

## Part 9: Common Affordability Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using Gross Salary Instead of Take-Home
```
WRONG: Gross ₹60k → Safe EMI = ₹9k
RIGHT: Take-home ₹44k → Safe EMI = ₹6.6k

Impact: Overestimating affordable EMI by ₹2.4k/month
```

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Existing Obligations
```
WRONG: Salary ₹50k → Max EMI = ₹7.5k
RIGHT: Salary ₹50k - home loan ₹18k - rent ₹8k = ₹24k left
       Max new EMI = ₹3.6k (to keep total <30%)

Impact: Overestimating by 2x
```

### Mistake 3: Not Accounting for Essential Expenses
```
WRONG: All leftover money = available for EMI
RIGHT: ₹50k take-home - ₹18k obligations - ₹18k essentials = ₹14k left

Impact: Huge surprise when rent/utilities come due
```

### Mistake 4: Trusting Claimed Mileage
```
WRONG: Claimed 20 km/l = ₹3k/month fuel cost
RIGHT: Real 14 km/l = ₹4.3k/month fuel cost

Impact: Underestimating fuel by ₹1.3k/month = ₹15.6k/year
```

### Mistake 5: Ignoring Insurance Costs
```
WRONG: EMI ₹40k = total car cost
RIGHT: EMI ₹40k + Insurance ₹1k + Maintenance ₹1.5k = ₹42.5k

Impact: Underestimating by 6%
```

## Part 10: Affordability Verdict Criteria

**GREEN LIGHT (Proceed)**:
- ✓ EMI ≤ 15% of take-home
- ✓ Total debt ≤ 30% of take-home
- ✓ Car price ≤ 1.5x annual salary
- ✓ Emergency fund ≥ 3 months
- ✓ Savings rate remains >10%
- ✓ Can explain and justify all numbers

**YELLOW LIGHT (Proceed with caution)**:
- ⚠ EMI 15-20% of take-home (one ratio stretches)
- ⚠ Multiple ratios at upper limits but not exceeding
- ⚠ Emergency fund exactly 3 months (no buffer)
- ⚠ Savings rate drops to 5-10%

**RED LIGHT (Don't proceed)**:
- ✗ EMI > 20% of take-home
- ✗ Total debt > 40% of take-home
- ✗ Car price > 2x annual salary
- ✗ Emergency fund < 3 months
- ✗ Multiple affordability ratios in danger
- ✗ Forced decision (timeline pressure, emotional reasons)
