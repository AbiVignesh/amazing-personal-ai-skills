# Car Buying Expert Skill — Core References

## Reference 1: Car Buying Mindset

### Principle 1: It's Not a Want, It's a Decision
**What to Avoid**:
- "I want this car" → emotional reasoning
- "This is the best car" → no car is "best," only "best for you"
- "Everyone has one" → social proof fallacy
- "I deserve it" → emotional justification for financial irresponsibility

**Better Framing**:
- "Does this car fit my life and budget?"
- "What am I trading off to buy this?"
- "Can I afford this *and* maintain financial health?"

---

### Principle 2: Variant Psychology
Why cars have so many variants:

```
PRICING LADDER:

Base Variant ₹15L
├─ Essential features only
├─ Targets budget-conscious buyers
├─ 15-20% of buyers choose this
└─ Manufacturer margin: ₹1.5L

Mid Variant ₹18L
├─ Base + comfort features (AC, power steering, ABS)
├─ Targets value-conscious buyers
├─ 60-65% of buyers choose this
└─ Manufacturer margin: ₹2.5L (higher margin!)

Top Variant ₹21L
├─ Mid + luxury features (leather, sunroof, advanced infotainment)
├─ Targets affluent buyers
├─ 15-20% of buyers choose this
└─ Manufacturer margin: ₹4L (highest margin!)

KEY INSIGHT:
Manufacturers make MORE profit on top variants.
So they heavily market top variants.
That doesn't mean they're better VALUE.

Example:
- Base Creta: ₹10 features for ₹15L
- Mid Creta: ₹14 features for ₹18L (₹0.29 per feature point)
- Top Creta: ₹17 features for ₹21L (₹1.24 per feature point!)

Mid-spec is almost always the best value.
Top-spec is for people who value features over cost.
Base-spec is usually too basic (saves money but costs comfort).
```

### Principle 3: EMI is Not the Whole Cost

**What Most People Count**:
- EMI: ₹20k/month
- Fuel: ₹3k/month
- **Total**: ₹23k/month

**What They Miss**:
- Insurance: ₹1k/month
- Maintenance: ₹1.5k/month (average over 5 years)
- Registration & taxes: ₹500/month (amortized)
- **Real Total**: ₹26k/month

**What They Completely Ignore**:
- Depreciation: Loss of ₹3L in year 1, ₹2.5L in years 2-3
- Opportunity cost of down payment
- Service delays (lost work time if stuck)
- Stress of financial pressure

---

### Principle 4: Resale Value is Critical for TCO

**Bad Resale Car** (e.g., luxury brand with poor brand equity):
```
Buy price: ₹50L
Resale after 5 years: ₹20L (40% retention)
5-year cost: ₹30L + insurance + maintenance
```

**Good Resale Car** (e.g., Maruti, Mahindra):
```
Buy price: ₹25L
Resale after 5 years: ₹14L (55%+ retention)
5-year cost: ₹11L + insurance + maintenance
```

The "cheap" car might be more expensive total cost of ownership if resale is poor.

### Principle 5: Fuel Type Matters in India

**Petrol**:
- Cost: ₹100/liter
- Mileage: 12-18 km/l (varies by car)
- Maintenance: Low
- Resale: Good
- Best for: <1000 km/month

**Diesel**:
- Cost: ₹88/liter
- Mileage: 15-22 km/l (better than petrol)
- Maintenance: Higher (DPF, higher service cost)
- Resale: Good (historically)
- Best for: >1500 km/month highway

**CNG** (petrol + gas dual fuel):
- Cost: ₹75/kg (cheapest)
- Mileage: 18-25 km/kg (excellent)
- Maintenance: Low
- Resale: Moderate (limited buyers)
- Boot space: Sacrificed (gas tank inside)
- Best for: High monthly mileage, city driving

**EV** (electric):
- Cost: ₹8-12/km (cheapest, if home charging)
- Mileage: 4-6 km/kWh
- Maintenance: Very low (no oil changes)
- Resale: Unknown (new in India)
- Charging: Requires home/office charging, limited charging network
- Best for: <100 km daily commute, home charging available

**Decision Framework**:
```
>1500 km/month?
├─ YES → Diesel OR EV (if home charging) OR CNG
├─ <500 km/month → Petrol (EV if budget allows)
└─ 500-1500 km/month → Either (petrol slightly cheaper)
```

### Principle 6: The Waiting Question

**When Waiting is Smart**:
- ✓ Facelift/new generation coming in <6 months (better features, longer service parts availability)
- ✓ Current model has reliability issues being fixed (wait for updated version)
- ✓ New segment being launched that fits you better
- ✓ You're not yet financially ready (waiting = save for larger down payment)

**When Waiting is Dumb**:
- ✗ Waiting indefinitely for "perfect car" (paralysis by analysis)
- ✗ Waiting for fuel prices to drop (they won't)
- ✗ Waiting for perfect interest rates (rates cycle, not predictable)
- ✗ Waiting because uncertain (paralysis, not strategy)

## Reference 2: India Car Market 2026

### Top Segments

**Hatchback** (₹5-10L): Maruti Alto, Tata Tiago, Hyundai i10
- Best for: City driving, small families, budget buyers
- Advantages: Cheap, fuel-efficient, easy to park, good resale
- Disadvantages: Small interior, poor crash safety, limited features

**Compact Sedan** (₹8-12L): Maruti Dzire, Tata Tigor, Honda Amaze
- Best for: Uber/taxi drivers, small families wanting sedan prestige
- Advantages: Boot space, sedan look, better features than hatchback
- Disadvantages: Less practical than hatchback, reliability mixed

**Compact SUV** (₹7-15L): Maruti Brezza, Hyundai Creta, Tata Nexon
- Best for: First-time car buyers, small families, style + practicality
- Advantages: High ground clearance (Indian roads), SUV look, decent features, good resale
- Disadvantages: Higher fuel cost, less legroom than sedan, poor highway comfort

**Mid-Size SUV** (₹15-25L): Hyundai Creta, Kia Seltos, MG Hector, Mahindra XUV500
- Best for: Growing families, some highway use, balance of comfort & space
- Advantages: More space, higher-spec features, better comfort, good resale
- Disadvantages: Fuel cost, maintenance cost, parking challenges

**Premium SUV** (₹25-50L): Fortuner, Endeavour, GLC
- Best for: Affluent families, serious highway use, luxury features
- Advantages: Best comfort, luxury features, resale (some models)
- Disadvantages: Expensive, high running costs, fuel consumption

**Sedan** (₹12-25L): Honda Accord, Toyota Camry, Maruti Ciaz
- Best for: Highway drivers, business professionals, comfort priority
- Advantages: Best highway comfort, boot space, premium feel
- Disadvantages: Higher fuel cost than hatchbacks, expensive

### EMI Affordability Rules (India-Specific)

**Safe Ratio**: EMI ≤ 15% of take-home salary
```
Salary: ₹50k/month take-home
Safe EMI: ₹7.5k
Max loan at 8% for 60 months: ₹30L (with ₹15-20L down payment)
Max car price: ₹45-50L on-road

Salary: ₹100k/month take-home
Safe EMI: ₹15k
Max loan at 8% for 60 months: ₹60L (with ₹20-30L down payment)
Max car price: ₹80-90L on-road
```

**Stretching Ratio**: EMI 15-20% of take-home (risky)
- Feasible temporarily, but if salary drops or emergency happens, you're in trouble
- Not recommended if savings rate drops below 10%

**Dangerous Ratio**: EMI > 20% of take-home
- HIGH RISK of default if any income disruption
- Eating into emergency fund
- Poor financial health

### Financing Options in India (2026)

**Bank Loans**:
- Interest: 7.5-9.5% depending on credit score
- Tenure: 36-84 months
- Down payment: 10-20% (negotiable)
- Processing: Slow (10-14 days)
- Best for: Creditworthy individuals

**NBFC Loans** (Non-Banking Financial Companies):
- Interest: 9-12% (higher than banks)
- Tenure: 36-84 months
- Down payment: 20-25%
- Processing: Fast (2-3 days)
- Best for: Salaried people with weak credit

**Dealer Financing**:
- Interest: 10-14% (often highest)
- Tenure: 36-84 months
- Down payment: 25%+
- Processing: Instant (at dealership)
- Risk: Tied to dealer, less consumer protection
- Best for: Convenience, instant approval

**Corporate FD-Backed Loans**:
- Interest: 6-7% (lowest)
- Tenure: 36-60 months
- Down payment: 0-10% (can be covered by FD)
- Best for: People with FDs available

**RULE**: Never take 84-month loan. Interest costs explode. 60 months is better.

## Reference 3: Cost per Feature Analysis

**Why This Matters**: Avoid paying for features you won't use

```
Example: Maruti Swift

Base Variant (₹5.9L):
- AC, power steering, ABS
- 6 features = ₹1.0L per feature

Standard (₹6.5L):
- Base + fog lights, power windows, keyless entry
- 9 features = ₹0.7L per feature ← BETTER VALUE

VXi (₹7.2L):
- Standard + alloy wheels, touchscreen infotainment
- 11 features = ₹0.65L per feature ← BEST VALUE

ZXi (₹8.0L):
- VXi + panoramic sunroof, leather seats, cruise control
- 14 features = ₹0.57L per feature

VERDICT: VXi is the sweet spot.
- ZXi costs ₹0.8L more
- Adds sunroof (rarely used in Indian heat) + leather (needs maintenance)
- Small decrease in per-feature cost doesn't justify ₹0.8L premium
```

**Identify Gimmick Features**:
- Panoramic sunroof: Doesn't add comfort, adds heat, needs cleaning, depreciates value
- Leather seats: Maintenance-heavy, uncomfortable in Indian heat
- Advanced infotainment: Updates slow, often unusable
- Ambient lighting: Zero utility, adds to cost
- Heated seats: Not needed in India
- Ventilated seats: Useful only in luxury segment with good AC

**Priority Features**:
- Safety: ABS, airbags, ESP (non-negotiable)
- Comfort: Good AC, power steering, power windows
- Practicality: Rear parking sensor, touchscreen with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto
- Convenience: Keyless entry, one-touch windows

## Reference 4: Real-World Fuel Mileage vs Claimed

**Claimed Mileage Multiplier**:
```
Claimed 20 km/l × 0.75 = 15 km/l real-world (for careful drivers)
Claimed 20 km/l × 0.70 = 14 km/l real-world (for average drivers)
Claimed 20 km/l × 0.65 = 13 km/l real-world (for aggressive drivers)

Example TCO Impact:
- Claimed: 18 km/l = ₹2,800/month fuel for 10,000 km/month
- Real: 13.5 km/l = ₹3,700/month fuel for 10,000 km/month
- Difference: ₹900/month = ₹10.8k/year = ₹54k over 5 years
```

**Factors Affecting Real Mileage**:
- Driver style: Aggressive acceleration/braking = -10-15%
- Traffic: City traffic = -20-30% vs highway
- AC usage: AC ON = -15-20%
- Vehicle age: Older vehicles = -10-15%
- Maintenance: Proper servicing = +5-10%
- Fuel quality: Poor quality = -5-10%

**Strategy**: Always use pessimistic mileage (0.70 multiplier) for financial calculations.

## Reference 5: Hidden Ownership Costs

**Often Forgotten**:

1. **Annual Insurance**: ₹5k-15k (varies by variant, location, driver age)
   - Third-party minimum (legal): ₹1.5-2k
   - Comprehensive (damage + theft): ₹8k-15k

2. **Registration & Road Tax**: ₹5-15k (one-time) + annual road tax ₹500-2k

3. **Maintenance Per Year**:
   - Year 1-3: ₹1,000-1,500 (servicing only)
   - Year 4-5: ₹2,000-3,000 (major service, filters, pads)
   - Year 5+: ₹3,000-5,000 (potential bigger repairs)

4. **Tyres**: Replacement every 4-5 years = ₹8-15k (₹200-300/month amortized)

5. **Battery**: Few cars need replacement, but if they do: ₹5-15k

6. **Parking**: In metros: ₹2-10k/month in buildings
   - Not direct cost, but affects lifestyle

7. **Toll & Permits**: ₹500-1,000/month if highway commute

8. **Washing & Detailing**: ₹500-1,000/month if regular

**TOTAL HIDDEN COSTS**: ₹2,000-4,000/month (on top of EMI + Fuel)
