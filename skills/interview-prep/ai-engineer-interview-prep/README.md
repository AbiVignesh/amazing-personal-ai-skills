# AI Engineer Interview Prep Skill

Production-ready interview preparation for top AI labs.

## Overview

This skill provides systematic, company-specific interview preparation for AI engineer roles at labs like Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Perplexity, ElevenLabs, and Sarvam AI.

**Key Philosophy:** No generic prep advice. Focus on real interview patterns, systems thinking, and shipping.

## Quick Start

Ask Claude any of these:

- "Am I ready for Anthropic/OpenAI-level roles?"
- "Give me a brutal assessment of my profile"
- "Mock interview for LLM systems design"
- "What projects should I build to stand out?"
- "Why am I getting rejected?"
- "Turn my experience into a strong AI resume"
- "Design a low-latency inference system for 1M concurrent users"
- "Which company should I target first?"

## How It Works

### 1. Assessment (Readiness Check)
Tell Claude about your background. Get an honest assessment:
- Current level (Early/Mid/Late stage)
- Technical depth gaps
- Shipping signal strength
- Timeline to readiness
- Company fit analysis

**Outputs:** Use `templates/assessment-report.md`

### 2. Personalized Roadmap
Get a week-by-week study plan tailored to:
- Your target companies
- Time available (4/8/12 weeks)
- Current gaps
- Learning style

**Includes:** Paper reading, coding drills, projects to ship, mock interview schedule

**Outputs:** Use `templates/roadmap-output.md`

### 3. Mock Interviews
Conduct realistic mocks in 4 modes:
- **Coding:** Real inference/systems problems (90 min)
- **ML Systems Design:** End-to-end system design (90 min)
- **LLM Design & Fine-tuning:** Tradeoff analysis (60 min)
- **Product & Research:** Communication & depth (45 min)

Get scored and brutally honest feedback.

**Outputs:** Use `templates/mock-interview-report.md`

### 4. Company-Specific Prep
Each lab hires for different signals:
- Anthropic: Reasoning + safety thinking
- OpenAI: Scale + inference speed
- DeepMind: Research + systems grounding
- Perplexity: Search + ranking + speed
- ElevenLabs: Real-time + audio
- Sarvam: Multilingual + pragmatism

**See:** `references/company-profiles.md`

### 5. Project Guidance
- What to build to stand out
- Portfolio critique
- Resume reframing
- Project quality rubric

**Outputs:** Use `templates/project-guidance.md`

## Workflows Supported

### Workflow 1: "I want to interview at Anthropic"
1. Assessment → understand gaps
2. Roadmap → 8-week plan (Anthropic-focused)
3. Mocks → weekly (reasoning + safety focus)
4. Projects → build something that signals fit
5. Resume → reframe to Anthropic signals

### Workflow 2: "Why am I getting rejected?"
1. Assessment → brutal honesty on why
2. Mock interviews → identify failure pattern
3. Targeted prep → fix the specific gap
4. Re-interview → try again with new signal

### Workflow 3: "I have X weeks, am I ready?"
1. Assessment → readiness level
2. Roadmap → accelerated or skip to mocks?
3. Mocks → gauge current performance
4. Polish → fix critical gaps
5. Apply → go for it

### Workflow 4: "Build my portfolio"
1. Project guidance → what to build for target company
2. Quality rubric → make sure it's strong
3. Resume reframe → turn projects into signals
4. Showcase → practice 2-min pitch

## Tone and Approach

- **Direct:** "You're not ready for Anthropic yet. Here's why."
- **Systems-focused:** "What's the bottleneck? What breaks at scale?"
- **Builder's mindset:** "Ship something real. Theory is secondary."
- **Pressure-realistic:** Mocks should feel like real interviews—hard, timed, with pushback
- **No sugarcoating:** Red flags get called out. Strengths get recognized. Gaps get prioritized.

## What Makes This Different

❌ **Avoid these patterns:**
- Generic LeetCode prep (wrong content)
- Credential-focused resume advice (doesn't signal)
- Breadth over depth (interviewers test depth)
- Disconnected theory (not connected to real systems)

✅ **This skill focuses on:**
- Real interview patterns from top labs
- Systems thinking + shipping signal
- Depth in 2-3 areas (not breadth)
- Company-specific hiring patterns
- Measurable interview readiness

## Success Markers

You're ready to interview when:
- [ ] Scoring consistently >70% on mocks
- [ ] 2+ shipped projects (each with learned lessons)
- [ ] Deep in 2+ core areas (can explain tradeoffs)
- [ ] Mock feedback shows consistent patterns (not new issues)
- [ ] Can pitch projects in 2 minutes, explaining impact
- [ ] Know company hiring signals + interview format
- [ ] Communication is clear and concise
