# FAANG Company-Specific Profiles

Each company hires differently. Know what they actually value before you interview.

---

## Google

**Hiring Signal:** Technical depth + problem-solving ability + systems thinking

**What they value:**
- Fundamental CS knowledge (algorithms, data structures)
- Ability to solve novel/hard problems
- Clean code, not clever code
- Communication and explaining reasoning
- Scaling thinking

**Interview Format:**
- **Phone/Video (1 round):** Medium DSA problem
- **Onsite (4 rounds):**
  - 2 coding rounds (1 medium, 1 hard DSA)
  - 1 system design round (HLD)
  - 1 behavioral round

**DSA Difficulty:** Medium to hard (not easy problems)

**System Design Difficulty:** High (expect 3-4 follow-ups)

**What breaks you:**
- Can't think out loud
- Over-complicating solutions
- Not asking clarifying questions
- Defensive when given hints

**What helps you:**
- Clear communication
- Asking good questions
- Considering edge cases
- Thinking about scalability early

**Company-Specific Focus:**
- Google cares about scale (billions of users)
- Complexity of problems matters
- Innovation within constraints

**Example Questions:**
- "Design YouTube" (expect deep dives on every component)
- "Design Google Search"
- "Design Gmail" (storage + features + scale)

---

## Amazon

**Hiring Signal:** Ownership + bias for action + operational excellence

**What they value:**
- Shipping mindset (done is better than perfect)
- Ownership of problems end-to-end
- Operational thinking (logs, monitoring, deployment)
- Amazon Leadership Principles (11 of them)
- Pragmatic solutions (cheap, fast)

**Interview Format:**
- **Phone (1 round):** Medium DSA
- **Onsite (4-5 rounds):**
  - 2 coding rounds (medium difficulty, sometimes with operational flavor)
  - 1 system design round (operational focus)
  - 1-2 behavioral rounds (they heavily weight this)
  - Possibly 1 bar raiser round (hardest)

**DSA Difficulty:** Medium (not as hard as Google)

**System Design Difficulty:** Medium (operational focus)

**What breaks you:**
- "Let me think about this more" (bias for action!)
- Designing perfect solutions (Amazon values speed)
- Not thinking about operations (logging, monitoring, deployment)
- Failing on behavioral rounds (40% of hiring decision)

**What helps you:**
- Quick decisions with reasoning
- Thinking about operations from the start
- Clear communication
- Showing ownership mindset

**Amazon Leadership Principles to highlight:**
1. Customer obsession
2. Ownership
3. Bias for action
4. Frugality (efficient design)
5. Learning and curious mindset

**Example Questions:**
- "Design an e-commerce system" (with operational focus)
- "Design a distributed cache" (with deployment concerns)
- "Design a job scheduler" (with monitoring + recovery)

## Meta (Facebook)

**Hiring Signal:** Move fast + impact + building at scale

**What they value:**
- Speed (ship fast, iterate)
- User impact (does it matter?)
- Willingness to take risks
- Practical problem-solving
- Comfortable with ambiguity

**Interview Format:**
- **Phone (1 round):** Easy-medium DSA
- **Onsite (4 rounds):**
  - 2 coding rounds (medium, focus on speed over perfect)
  - 1 system design round (practical focus)
  - 1 behavioral round

**DSA Difficulty:** Easy to medium (lower than Google/Amazon)

**System Design Difficulty:** Medium (practical not perfect)

**What breaks you:**
- Perfectionism (ship instead)
- Overly cautious approach
- Defensive about "quick and dirty" code
- Not understanding user impact

**What helps you:**
- Pragmatism (MVP thinking)
- Speed of decision-making
- Impact orientation
- Comfortable with iteration

**Meta-Specific Focus:**
- Move fast mindset
- Growth at scale (billions of users)
- Continuous deployment capability
- Feedback loops matter

**Example Questions:**
- "Design Instagram feed" (focus on speed + iteration)
- "Design live video streaming" (real-time systems)
- "Design a recommendation system" (impact-focused)

## Apple

**Hiring Signal:** Attention to quality + systems thinking + communication

**What they value:**
- Clean, high-quality code
- Attention to detail
- Understanding the "why" behind decisions
- System-level thinking
- Clear communication

**Interview Format:**
- **Phone (1 round):** Medium DSA, strong communication focus
- **Onsite (4 rounds):**
  - 2 coding rounds (medium, but code quality matters heavily)
  - 1 system design round (quality + practical)
  - 1 behavioral round

**DSA Difficulty:** Medium

**System Design Difficulty:** Medium (quality-focused)

**What breaks you:**
- Sloppy code (Apple cares about quality)
- Not explaining your reasoning
- "It works" without understanding why
- Dismissing edge cases

**What helps you:**
- Clean, readable code
- Explaining every design decision
- Asking clarifying questions
- Understanding user impact

**Apple-Specific Focus:**
- Quality and craft matter
- System coherence (everything fits together)
- Attention to detail
- Understanding the user experience

**Example Questions:**
- "Design a music streaming system" (quality + experience focus)
- "Design macOS boot process" (systems thinking)
- "Design a health monitoring app" (user-centric)

---

## Netflix

**Hiring Signal:** Freedom and responsibility + solving hard problems

**What they value:**
- Autonomous problem-solving
- Comfort with ambiguity
- No process (you own it)
- Creative solutions
- Technical judgment

**Interview Format:**
- **Phone (1 round):** Medium-hard DSA (sometimes open-ended)
- **Onsite (4 rounds):**
  - 2 coding rounds (medium, may have ambiguous requirements)
  - 1 system design round (dealing with ambiguity)
  - 1 behavioral round

**DSA Difficulty:** Medium to hard

**System Design Difficulty:** Hard (you set your own requirements)

**What breaks you:**
- Asking for permission (they want autonomy)
- Not making decisions under ambiguity
- Following process too rigidly
- Over-thinking before acting

**What helps you:**
- Making assumptions clearly stated
- Comfortable with ambiguity
- Quick iteration
- Clear communication of choices

**Netflix-Specific Focus:**
- Freedom and responsibility culture
- You're hired to think, not follow orders
- Make decisions quickly with incomplete information
- Communication through candor (honest, direct)

**Example Questions:**
- "Design Netflix recommendation system" (ambiguous, you decide)
- "Design a microservices architecture for Netflix" (you decide structure)
- "How would you improve Netflix?" (open-ended)

## Microsoft, LinkedIn, etc. (Tier-1 Adjacent)

**Similar to:** Combination of Google + Amazon + Meta

**What they value:**
- Technical competence (Google-like)
- Practical shipping (Amazon-like)
- Some speed (Meta-like)

**Interview Format:**
- Typically 4-5 rounds
- 2-3 coding, 1 system design, 1 behavioral

**Difficulty:** Medium to hard (varies by team)

## Universal Red Flags (All Companies)

These will disqualify you at any FAANG:

1. **Can't communicate:** Rambling, can't explain clearly
2. **Doesn't listen:** Don't ask clarifying questions
3. **Gets defensive:** "My code is fine" when questioned
4. **Slow thinking:** Can't solve medium problem in 45 min
5. **No edge cases:** Forgets to handle nulls, empty input, etc.
6. **Ignores hints:** Interviewer gives hint, you ignore it
7. **No follow-up questions:** Don't ask about tradeoffs or details
8. **Story doesn't match the question:** (behavioral)

---

## Universal Green Flags (All Companies)

These help at any FAANG:

1. **Clear communication:** Explain before coding
2. **Asks great questions:** "Can I assume...? What about...?"
3. **Iterates:** "Here's the naive approach, here's optimized"
4. **Handles feedback:** Takes hints, adjusts quickly
5. **Edge cases first:** Considers null, empty, large scale
6. **Code quality:** Readable, not clever
7. **Time management:** Paces yourself well
8. **Growth mindset:** "I didn't know that, here's how I'd figure it out"

## Targeting Strategy

**Start with:** Meta (lower difficulty, good to warm up)
**Then:** Amazon (medium difficulty + behavioral heavy)
**Then:** Google (hard DSA)
**Then:** Apple (quality-focused)
**Then:** Netflix (ambiguity + hard)

Or target the company you want most and prep specifically for them.
