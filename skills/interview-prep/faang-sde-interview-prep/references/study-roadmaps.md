# Study Roadmaps for FAANG SDE Interviews

Concrete 4/8/12 week preparation plans. Choose based on your current level and time available.

---

## 4-Week Crash Course (Target: Get Interview-Ready)

**For:** Intermediate engineers or those with some DSA knowledge. Time commitment: 60-80 hours.

**Assumption:** You know basic data structures (arrays, strings, lists) but DSA is rusty.

### Week 1: Foundation + Pattern 1-3

**Goal:** Get back into DSA mindset, master 3 core patterns

**Daily (Mon-Fri): 2.5-3 hours**

- **Mon-Tue (2.5h):** Sliding window pattern
  - Read DSA pattern map section
  - Code 3 problems: max consecutive ones, longest substring without repeating, minimum window substring
  - Time each problem (should get faster)

- **Wed-Fri (2.5h each):** Two pointers + Fast & slow
  - Code 3 two-pointer problems
  - Code 2 fast & slow problems (linked list cycle, palindrome)
  - Focus on understanding, not just coding

**Weekend (3h):** Mock coding round
- Pick a medium problem (not practiced)
- Time yourself: 45 minutes
- Score yourself: correctness (80%), speed (20%)
- Review: Did you explain? Did you handle edge cases?

**Milestones:**
- [ ] 3 sliding window problems solved and explained
- [ ] 3 two pointer problems solved
- [ ] 2 fast & slow pointer problems solved
- [ ] 1 timed mock done

**Expected Speed:** 30-40 min per medium problem by Friday

---

### Week 2: Patterns 4-6 + Practice

**Goal:** Add 3 more patterns, increase speed

**Daily (Mon-Fri): 2.5-3 hours**

- **Mon-Tue (2.5h):** Merge intervals + Cyclic sort
  - 3 merge interval problems
  - 3 cyclic sort problems

- **Wed-Fri (2.5h each):** In-place reversal + Speed drills
  - 2 in-place reversal problems
  - 5 speed drills on weeks 1-2 patterns (3 easy, 2 medium)
  - Time each: can you do easy in 15 min? Medium in 30 min?

**Weekend (3h):** Full mock round
- Medium problem (not practiced)
- 45 min time limit
- Score: correctness, speed, communication

**Milestones:**
- [ ] 6 new patterns done
- [ ] Speed on easy problems: <15 min
- [ ] Speed on medium: 30-40 min
- [ ] 2 timed mocks completed

---

### Week 3: Trees/Graphs + System Design Basics

**Goal:** Add graph/tree patterns, intro to system design

**Daily (Mon-Fri): 2.5-3 hours**

- **Mon-Tue (2.5h):** Tree BFS/DFS
  - 3 tree BFS problems
  - 3 tree DFS problems
  - Focus on understanding traversal order

- **Wed-Fri (2.5h each):** Graph patterns + System design intro
  - Wed: Graph BFS/DFS (3 problems)
  - Thu: Intro to system design (read frameworks, don't design yet)
  - Fri: Speed drills on all patterns so far

**Weekend (3h):** 2 mocks
- 1 coding mock (hard problem, 50 min)
- 1 baby system design (small scope, 30 min)

**Milestones:**
- [ ] Tree/graph patterns understood
- [ ] Can solve hard problem in 50 min
- [ ] System design basics known
- [ ] Speed drills consistent

---

### Week 4: Wrap-up + Company-Specific Prep

**Goal:** Polish weaknesses, do company-specific prep

**Daily (Mon-Fri): 2-2.5 hours**

- **Mon-Tue (2.5h):** Target company prep
  - Study company profiles
  - Read 2-3 interview reports from that company
  - Do 2 company-specific medium problems

- **Wed-Fri (2h each):** Full mock interviews
  - Wed: Full mock (coding 45 min + system design 30 min)
  - Thu: Behavioral mock (STAR method, 30 min)
  - Fri: Final mock or weakness drilling

**Weekend:** Review + apply

**Milestones:**
- [ ] Know target company's interview format
- [ ] 4+ full mocks completed
- [ ] Consistent scoring (>70%)
- [ ] Ready to interview

## 8-Week Deep Dive (Target: Compete with Strong Candidates)

**For:** Early-career engineers or 6+ months to prepare. Time commitment: 100-120 hours.

### Weeks 1-2: Foundation (All patterns 1-6)

- Week 1: Patterns 1-3 (sliding window, two pointers, fast & slow)
- Week 2: Patterns 4-6 (merge intervals, cyclic sort, in-place reversal)

**Problems:** 15-20 total
**Speed goal:** Easy in 15 min, medium in 35 min

---

### Weeks 3-4: Data Structures (Trees, Graphs, more)

- Week 3: Tree patterns (BFS, DFS)
- Week 4: Graph patterns (BFS, DFS) + Linked list problems

**Problems:** 20-25 total
**Mock schedule:** 2 mocks per week

---

### Weeks 5-6: Advanced DSA (DP, Backtracking, Bit)

- Week 5: Dynamic programming (easy to medium)
  - Climbing stairs, house robber, coin change
  - 10-15 problems

- Week 6: Backtracking (medium to hard)
  - Permutations, combinations, N-queens
  - 10-15 problems

**Speed goal:** Medium in 40-45 min, hard in 50-60 min

---

### Weeks 7-8: System Design + Behavioral

- Week 7: System design deep dive
  - HLD: Design Instagram, design Uber, design Twitter
  - Do 2-3 full designs (60 min each)
  - Record yourself, listen back

- Week 8: Behavioral + final mocks
  - Prepare 4-5 STAR stories
  - Company-specific cultural prep
  - 2 full interview mocks (coding + system design + behavioral)

---

## 12-Week Mastery (Target: Stand Out, Multiple Offers)

**For:** Those wanting to dominate, have 3+ months. Time commitment: 180-200 hours.

### Month 1: Foundation + Building Speed

**Weeks 1-2:** All 6 core patterns
**Weeks 3-4:** Trees, graphs, linked lists, plus speed drills

**Milestones:**
- [ ] All patterns understood deeply
- [ ] Can solve medium in 30-35 min
- [ ] Easy in <15 min

---

### Month 2: Advanced + Breadth

**Weeks 5-6:** Dynamic programming
- Coin change family (10 problems)
- House robber family (10 problems)
- LCS, LIS, edit distance (10 problems)
- Total DP problems: 30+

**Weeks 7-8:** Backtracking + Other patterns
- Permutations, combinations, subsets
- Topological sort, union find
- Greedy, bit manipulation
- 25+ problems total

**Milestones:**
- [ ] DP intuition developed
- [ ] Can recognize DP problems
- [ ] Hard problems in 55-60 min

---

### Month 3: System Design + Interview Simulation

**Weeks 9-10:** System design mastery
- Design 10+ systems (Instagram, Twitter, Uber, Airbnb, Dropbox, etc.)
- HLD focus (weeks 9-10)
- LLD on 2-3 projects (weeks 9-10)
- Record all designs

**Weeks 11-12:** Full interview simulation
- Week 11: 5 full mocks (coding + system design + behavioral each)
- Week 12: Polish + apply

**Milestones:**
- [ ] 10+ systems designed confidently
- [ ] 5+ full interview mocks done
- [ ] Scoring >80% on mocks
- [ ] Company-specific prep done

## Daily Schedule Template

**For 8-week roadmap, example day:**

**Morning (8-10am): 2 hours**
- 1 problem from target pattern (40 min with explanation)
- Review solution + test edge cases (20 min)

**Afternoon (2-4pm): 2 hours**
- 1 speed drill on previous pattern (20 min)
- 1 new problem or system design reading (40 min)
- Review and note learnings (20 min)

**Evening (optional, 1h):**
- Mock interview or review difficult problem

**Weekend:**
- 1 full mock interview (timed, scored)
- Debrief and identify weak areas

---

## Difficulty Progression

**Weeks 1-3:** Easy → easy-medium
**Weeks 4-6:** Medium → medium-hard
**Weeks 7-8:** Hard → mixed (build speed on harder problems)

---

## Weekly Check-in

Each week, rate yourself:

- **Speed:** Can you solve problems in the target time?
- **Correctness:** First-pass success rate? Edge cases?
- **Communication:** Can you explain while coding?
- **Confidence:** Do you recognize patterns quickly?

If any area is weak, adjust next week's focus.

## Company-Specific Roadmap Adjustments

### For Google:
- Weeks 1-4: Standard roadmap
- Weeks 5-6: Emphasis on hard problems
- Weeks 7-8: System design with deep dives
- Tip: Practice explaining complex tradeoffs

### For Amazon:
- Weeks 1-4: Standard roadmap
- Weeks 5-6: Medium emphasis (not as many hard)
- Weeks 7-8: Behavioral (40% of hiring), system design with operational focus
- Tip: Practice STAR stories, leadership principles

### For Meta:
- Weeks 1-4: Standard roadmap
- Weeks 5-6: Medium problems (not hard)
- Weeks 7-8: Speed emphasis, quick decisions
- Tip: Focus on moving fast, practical solutions
